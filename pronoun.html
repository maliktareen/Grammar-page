What Is a Pronoun? | Definition, Types & Examples

A pronoun is a word that stands in for a noun, often to avoid the need to repeat the same noun over and over. Like nouns, pronouns can refer to people, things, concepts, and places. Most sentences contain at least one noun or pronoun. People tend to use “pronouns” to mean personal pronouns specifically, but there are many other kinds of pronouns that are just as important to English grammar. The words highlighted in bold below are all pronouns.

Exaples: pronouns

Subjective Pronoun:

Definition:Subjective Pronouns used as the subject of a sentence (the doer of the action).
Examples: I, You, He, She, It, We, They

Objective pronoun

Definition:Objective Pronouns used as the object of a verb or preposition (receiver of the action).
Examples: Me, You, Him, Her, It, Us, Them

Possessive pronoun

Definition: Pronouns that show ownership but stand alone without a noun.
Examples: Mine, Yours, His, Hers, Ours, Theirs.

Possessive Adjective pronoun

Definition: Words that show ownership and come before a noun.
Examples: My, Your, His, Her, Its, Our, Their

Reflexive Pronouns

Definition: Pronouns used when the subject and object are the same person/thing.
Examples: Myself, Yourself, Himself, Herself, Itself, Ourselves, Yourselves, Themselves
